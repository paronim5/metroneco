About Author

Dmitry Glukhovsky is a Russian writer, playwright and journalist.

Dmitry Glukhovsky’s debut novel, METRO 2033, is a post-apocalyptic dystopia set in the Moscow metro system after World War III. It began in 2002 as a free online project and, five years later, became a global bestseller. The book has since been translated into 40 languages and sold over 3 million copies worldwide. It also inspired the cult-favorite METRO video game franchise, helping establish Glukhovsky as a pioneer in transmedia storytelling. His later works—METRO 2034, METRO 2035, SUMERKI, FUTURE, and TEXT—also started online before gaining international acclaim. He wrote the screenplay for the feature film adaptation of TEXT, created the TV series TOPI (also known as The Quagmires), and penned the play THE WHITE FACTORY, which premiered at the Marylebone Theatre in London.As a television journalist, Glukhovsky reported from around the globe—covering stories in Morocco, Guatemala, Iceland, and Japan. He filmed inside the Chernobyl exclusion zone, witnessed rocket launches in Baikonur, reported from under rocket fire in Israel’s Kiryat Shmona, and even broadcast the first-ever live report from the North Pole.In addition to his native Russian, he speaks English, French, German, Hebrew, and Spanish fluently.A vocal critic of Russia’s authoritarian government, Glukhovsky openly condemned Vladimir Putin’s invasion of Ukraine in 2022. In response, Russian authorities put him on a wanted list for his outspoken articles and interviews. In August 2023, a Moscow court sentenced him in absentia to eight and a half years in prison for his anti-war stance. He currently lives in exile somewhere in Europe.
